Omnipair's liquidation mechanism fundamentally differs from traditional DeFi lending protocols. Instead of relying on auctions or external price oracles, Omnipair uses **impact-aware collateral valuation** with **pessimistic EMA pricing** to determine liquidation thresholds and execute debt write-offs.

## Impact-Based Collateral Valuation

Traditional lending protocols value collateral linearly:

$$
V_{\text{linear}} = \text{Collateral} \times P_{\text{oracle}}
$$

This approach fails to account for the **price impact** of actually liquidating that collateral through the AMM. Omnipair instead calculates collateral value by simulating the swap:

$$
V_{\text{impact}} = \text{CPCurve.calculate\_amount\_out}(R_{\text{collateral}}, R_{\text{debt}}, \text{Collateral})
$$

Using the constant product formula:

$$
\Delta y = \frac{\Delta x \cdot y}{x + \Delta x}
$$

This ensures the protocol never overestimates what it can actually recover by selling collateral.

### Why Impact Matters

For small positions, linear and impact valuations are similar. But as collateral size increases relative to pool reserves, price impact becomes significant:

| Collateral (% of Reserve) | Linear Value | Impact Value | Difference |
|---------------------------|--------------|--------------|------------|
| 1% | 10,000 | 9,901 | -1% |
| 10% | 100,000 | 90,909 | -9% |
| 50% | 500,000 | 333,333 | -33% |

Without impact-aware valuation, large positions could borrow more than their collateral can actually cover in liquidation.

## Pessimistic EMA Pricing

Before calculating collateral value, Omnipair reconstructs **virtual reserves at the pessimistic price** rather than using spot reserves directly.

### Double-EMA System

Omnipair maintains two EMAs for each token price:

**1. Symmetric EMA** — Standard two-way smoothing (configurable half-life: 1 min to 12 hours):

$$
\text{EMA}_{\text{sym}}(t) = \alpha \cdot P_{\text{spot}} + (1 - \alpha) \cdot \text{EMA}_{\text{sym}}(t-1)
$$

**2. Directional EMA** — Asymmetric behavior (fixed 3-second half-life):
- **On price drop:** Snaps down instantly → $\text{EMA}_{\text{dir}} = \min(\text{EMA}_{\text{dir}}, P_{\text{spot}})$
- **On price rise:** Rises gradually via standard EMA calculation

### Pessimistic Price

The protocol uses the more conservative of the two EMAs:

$$
P_{\text{pessimistic}} = \min(P_{\text{directional}}, P_{\text{symmetric}})
$$

This ensures:
- Rapid price drops are captured immediately (directional EMA snaps down)
- Price manipulation via temporary spikes is ineffective (must sustain for ~50 slots)
- Collateral is always valued conservatively

### Constructing Virtual Reserves

Virtual reserves are reconstructed at the pessimistic price while preserving the AMM invariant $k = x \cdot y$:

$$
x_{\text{virtual}} = \sqrt{\frac{k}{P_{\text{pessimistic}}}}
$$

$$
y_{\text{virtual}} = \sqrt{k \cdot P_{\text{pessimistic}}}
$$

These virtual reserves are then used for all collateral valuation and CF calculations, ensuring consistency between borrowing limits and liquidation thresholds.

## Liquidation Mechanism

### Liquidation Condition

A position is liquidatable when debt exceeds the borrow limit:

$$
\text{Debt} \geq V_{\text{impact}} \times \text{Liquidation CF}
$$

Where $V_{\text{impact}}$ is calculated using virtual reserves at the pessimistic price.

### Partial vs Full Liquidation

**Solvent positions** (collateral value > debt): Partial liquidation using close factor
- Only a portion of debt is written off per liquidation call
- Prevents over-liquidation of recoverable positions

**Insolvent positions** (debt > collateral value): Full debt write-off
- All remaining debt is written off
- Bad debt is socialized to LPs through reduced pool reserves

### Collateral Seizure

When liquidation occurs:

1. **Base collateral** is calculated with price impact:
   $$
   \text{Collateral}_{\text{base}} = \text{CPCurve.calculate\_amount\_in}(R_{\text{collateral}}, R_{\text{debt}}, \text{Debt to writeoff})
   $$

2. **Liquidation penalty** is added (paid by borrower, benefits LPs):
   $$
   \text{Collateral}_{\text{total}} = \text{Collateral}_{\text{base}} \times (1 + \text{Penalty BPS} / 10000)
   $$

3. **Liquidator incentive** is paid from the base amount:
   $$
   \text{Incentive} = \text{Collateral}_{\text{base}} \times \text{Incentive BPS} / 10000
   $$

4. **Remaining collateral** goes to pool reserves (LPs benefit from penalty minus incentive)

### Flow of Funds

```
Seized Collateral
    ├── Liquidator Incentive → Caller's token account
    └── Remainder → Pool reserves (cash_reserve + reserve)
```

## Collateral Factor System

### Two CF Values

The system maintains two distinct collateral factors:

| CF Type | Purpose | Relationship |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| **Liquidation CF** | Threshold for liquidation | Base CF |
| **Borrow CF** | Maximum for new borrows | Liquidation CF × 0.95 |

The 5% LTV buffer ensures users have time to add collateral or repay before liquidation.

### Dynamic vs Fixed CF

**Dynamic CF** (default): Calculated from AMM curve, accounts for:
- Price impact of liquidating collateral
- Existing pool debt utilization
- Capped at 85% maximum

**Fixed CF** (optional): Pool-specific override for stable pairs or special use cases.

### Applied Min CF

Each user position stores `applied_min_cf_bps` — the CF at the time of borrowing. This protects users:

$$
\text{Effective CF} = \max(\text{Current CF}, \text{Applied Min CF})
$$

If pool conditions cause CF to decrease after borrowing, users keep their original CF until they interact with the position again.

## Security Properties

### Manipulation Resistance

The pessimistic pricing system ensures:

1. **Spot price manipulation** cannot increase borrowing capacity (uses EMA, not spot)
2. **Large positions** cannot borrow more than liquidation can recover (impact-aware valuation)
3. **Split attacks** are mitigated (total borrowable across split accounts ≤ single account)

### Invariant: Borrow Limit ≤ Liquidation Limit

Both borrowing and liquidation use the same valuation method:

$$
\text{Borrow Limit} = V_{\text{impact}} \times \text{Borrow CF}
$$

$$
\text{Liquidation Limit} = V_{\text{impact}} \times \text{Liquidation CF}
$$

Since Borrow CF = Liquidation CF × 0.95, the borrow limit is always strictly below the liquidation threshold for any collateral size.

## Advantages

- **No oracle dependency**: All pricing derived from on-chain AMM state with EMA smoothing
- **Impact-aware**: Large positions automatically get lower effective CF
- **Manipulation-resistant**: Pessimistic pricing prevents front-running and price manipulation
- **Permissionless liquidation**: Anyone can call liquidate, incentivized by liquidator reward
- **LP protection**: Liquidation penalty flows to LPs, partially offsetting bad debt

## Summary

Omnipair's liquidation system combines:

| Component | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **Double-EMA** | Manipulation-resistant price smoothing |
| **Pessimistic pricing** | Conservative collateral valuation |
| **Impact-aware CF** | Accounts for actual liquidation slippage |
| **Partial liquidation** | Prevents over-liquidation of solvent positions |
| **Debt write-off** | Immediate bad debt resolution without auctions |
