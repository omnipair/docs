Omnipair is powered by a Generalized Automated Market Maker (GAMM), a primitive that combines constant-product AMM functionality with isolated lending logic. This allows a single pool to support both spot trading and margin borrowing without oracles or centralized risk config.

Each pool holds a token pair and provides:

- Swap functionality via spot prices
- Margin borrowing based on time-weighted EMA prices
- Interest accrual via utilization-based dynamic rates
- Liquidation through debt write-offs

## Pool Structure

Each pool is defined by two tokens ($x$, $y$). Liquidity is added in a constant product fashion where $xy = k$, and reserves are tracked using a three-tier accounting system:

| Reserve Type | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Virtual Reserve** | Used for AMM pricing and swap execution |
| **Cash Reserve** | Actual available liquidity in vaults |
| **Debt** | Borrowed amounts outstanding |

These are related by: $R_{\text{virtual}} = R_{\text{cash}} + R_{\text{debt}}$

The protocol enforces the **solvency invariant**: $R_{\text{virtual}} \geq R_{\text{debt}}$, ensuring cash reserves never go negative.

### Spot Price

Derived from the pool's token virtual reserves:

$$
\text{Spot Price}_{0 \rightarrow 1} = \frac{R^{\text{virtual}}_1}{R^{\text{virtual}}_0}
$$

Used for swap execution following the constant-product formula:

$$
\Delta y = \frac{\Delta x \cdot y}{x + \Delta x}
$$

### Double-EMA Price System

Omnipair uses a **double-EMA system** for lending operations:

**1. Symmetric EMA** — Standard two-way smoothing with configurable half-life (1 min to 12 hours):
$$
\text{EMA}_{\text{sym}}(t) = \alpha \cdot P_{\text{spot}} + (1 - \alpha) \cdot \text{EMA}_{\text{sym}}(t-1)
$$

**2. Directional EMA** — Asymmetric: snaps down instantly on price drops, rises gradually (3-second half-life):
$$
\text{EMA}_{\text{dir}} = \begin{cases} \min(\text{EMA}_{\text{dir}}, P_{\text{spot}}) & \text{if price drops} \\ \text{EMA calculation} & \text{if price rises} \end{cases}
$$

**Pessimistic Price** — Used for all collateral calculations:
$$
P_{\text{pessimistic}} = \min(P_{\text{directional}}, P_{\text{symmetric}})
$$

This design ensures the protocol reacts immediately to adverse price movements while preventing manipulation through temporary price spikes.

## Borrowing & Collateralization

Each user can deposit either Token0 or Token1 as collateral. Debt is tracked per user using debt shares, ensuring proportional interest accrual.

Let:

- $C_0$: collateral in Token0
- $D_1$: debt in Token1
- $( CF )$: collateral factor (e.g. 85%)

Then borrowing is allowed if:

$$
D_1 \cdot \text{EMA}_{1 \rightarrow 0} \leq C_0 \cdot CF
$$

Solvency is checked against EMA prices, not spot, to prevent manipulation.

## Interest Rate Model

Each pool uses a fully parameterizable dynamic interest rate model that adjusts based on utilization:

$$
U = \frac{\text{TotalDebt}}{\text{Reserve}_{\text{virtual}}}
$$

### Rate Model Parameters

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| `target_util_start` | Lower bound of optimal utilization range |
| `target_util_end` | Upper bound of optimal utilization range |
| `half_life_ms` | Rate adjustment speed (lower = faster) |
| `min_rate` | Minimum rate floor |
| `max_rate` | Maximum rate ceiling (0 = no cap) |
| `initial_rate` | Starting rate for new pairs |

### Utilization Bands

- **Low** ($U <$ `target_util_start`): Rate decays exponentially toward `min_rate`
- **Optimal** (`target_util_start` $\leq U \leq$ `target_util_end`): Rate stays flat
- **High** ($U >$ `target_util_end`): Rate grows exponentially, capped at `max_rate`

### Rate Evolution

$$
r(t) = r(t-1) \cdot e^{\pm k \cdot \Delta t}
$$

Where $k = \ln(2) / \text{half\_life\_ms}$

### Interest Accrual

Interest is calculated using the integral of the rate function:

$$
\text{Interest} = \text{Debt} \times \text{Integral}
$$

$$
\text{Integral} = \frac{r_{\text{end}} - r_{\text{start}}}{k} \div \text{MILLISECONDS\_PER\_YEAR}
$$

### Protocol Fee

- Borrowers pay: `interest + protocol_fee`
- LPs receive: Full interest amount
- Protocol receives: `protocol_fee` (additive, not deducted from LP share)

The rate is updated on any pool interaction (`swap`, `borrow`, `repay`, etc.).

## Fee Surplus Accounting

Protocol fees are tracked **implicitly** through the difference between actual vault balances and accounted cash reserves:

$$
\text{Fee Surplus} = R_{\text{vault}} - R_{\text{cash}}
$$

This surplus represents claimable protocol revenue that flows to the futarchy governance system.

### Fee Sources

**1. Swap Fees** — Split between LPs and protocol:

$$
\text{Futarchy Fee} = \text{Swap Fee} \times \frac{\text{swap\_bps}}{10000}
$$

The futarchy portion is transferred immediately to the authority account, while the LP portion increases both virtual and cash reserves.

**2. Interest Protocol Fees** — Additive fee on borrower interest:

$$
\text{Protocol Fee} = \text{Interest} \times \frac{\text{interest\_bps}}{10000}
$$

### Cash Coverage Mechanism

When interest accrues, the protocol handles fees carefully to maintain the solvency invariant:

$$
\text{Cash Covered Fee} = \min(\text{Protocol Fee}, R_{\text{cash}})
$$

- **Covered portion**: Reduces cash reserves, creating immediate surplus in vaults
- **Uncovered portion**: Added to virtual reserves; LPs get temporary claim until borrowers repay

This ensures the state transition equation $\Delta R_{\text{virtual}} = \Delta R_{\text{cash}} + \Delta R_{\text{debt}}$ holds even when cash reserves are low.

### Fee Distribution

The accumulated surplus is distributed via the futarchy governance system to:
- Futarchy treasury
- Buybacks vault  
- Team treasury

## Liquidation & Solvency Enforcement

There are no auctions or instant liquidations. Instead, when a borrower exceeds their limit:

1. Their position is marked as insolvent.
2. Their debt is written off (removed from the debt pool).

This model avoids large price impacts, frontrunning, and incentivizes safe borrowing by LPs and traders alike.

Liquidation protection logic ensures:

- Pool stays solvent even if collateral drops in value.
- LPs do not suffer withdrawal failures due to shortfall in reserves.

## Recursive Leverage (Looping)

To take leveraged positions on a token (say $\text{Pair}_{\text{base}}$), users can:

1. Deposit $\text{Pair}_{\text{base}}$ as collateral.
2. Borrow $\text{Pair}_{\text{quote}}$.
3. Swap $\text{Pair}_{\text{quote}}$ → $\text{Pair}_{\text{base}}$.
4. Repeat steps 1–3.

This loop effectively increases exposure to $\text{Pair}_{\text{base}}$, achieving leverage.

Leverage achievable is capped by the collateral factor $(CF)$:

$$
\text{Max Leverage} = \frac{1}{1 - CF}
$$

Example with $( CF = 0.85 )$:

$$
\text{Max Leverage} = \frac{1}{1 - 0.85} = 6.67\times
$$

This model is similar to recursive borrowing in protocols like Compound or Aave, but is achieved here within a single isolated pool, avoiding cross-asset risk.

## Summary

| Mechanism | Implementation |
| --------- | -------------- |
| **Pricing** | Spot (for swaps), Double-EMA with pessimistic pricing (for lending) |
| **Solvency** | Three-tier reserves with state transition invariant |
| **Liquidations** | Debt write-off with price impact calculation |
| **Interest Rates** | Fully parameterizable exponential model with min/max caps |
| **Borrowing Model** | Isolated per pool with impact-aware collateral factors |
| **Leverage** | Recursive, within single pool |
| **Fee Accounting** | Implicit surplus tracking; fees flow to futarchy governance |
| **Oracles** | Not required |

Omnipair combines capital efficiency with safety by avoiding systemic liquidation risks, external oracles, and governance bottlenecks.
