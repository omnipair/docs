Omnipair is powered by a Generalized Automated Market Maker (GAMM), a primitive that combines constant-product AMM functionality with isolated lending logic. This allows a single pool to support both spot trading and margin borrowing without oracles or centralized risk configuration.

Each pool holds a token pair and provides:

- Swap functionality via spot prices
- Margin borrowing based on time-weighted EMA prices
- Interest accrual via utilization-based dynamic rates
- Liquidation through debt write-offs

Each pool is defined by two tokens ($x$, $y$). Liquidity is added in a constant product fashion where $xy = k$, and reserves are tracked using a three-tier accounting system.

## Reserve Types

Each pool maintains three distinct reserve values per token:

| Reserve Type | Description | Usage |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| **Virtual Reserve** (`reserve`) | Used for AMM pricing | Spot price calculation, swap execution |
| **Cash Reserve** (`cash_reserve`) | Actual available liquidity in vaults | Withdrawal limits, borrowing limits |
| **Debt** (`total_debt`) | Borrowed amounts outstanding | Interest accrual, solvency checks |

These reserves are related by the fundamental equation:

$$
R_{\text{virtual}} = R_{\text{cash}} + R_{\text{debt}}
$$

## Solvency Invariant

The protocol maintains strict invariants to ensure pool solvency:

**1. Constant Product Invariant:**
$$
x_{\text{virtual}} \times y_{\text{virtual}} = k
$$

**2. Solvency Invariant:**
$$
R_{\text{virtual}} \geq R_{\text{debt}} \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_{\text{cash}} \geq 0
$$

**3. State Transition Equation:**

For every operation, the following must hold:
$$
\Delta R_{\text{virtual}} = \Delta R_{\text{cash}} + \Delta R_{\text{debt}}
$$

| Operation | ΔR_virtual | ΔR_cash | ΔR_debt |
|-----------|------------|---------|---------|
| Borrow | 0 | −amount | +amount |
| Repay | 0 | +amount | −amount |
| Swap | +lp_fee | +lp_fee | 0 |
| Add Liquidity | +amount | +amount | 0 |
| Remove Liquidity | −amount | −amount | 0 |
| Interest Accrual | +lp_share + uncovered_fee | −covered_fee | +total_cost |

## Fee Surplus Accounting

Protocol fees are tracked **implicitly** as the difference between actual vault token balances and the accounted cash reserves:

$$
\text{Fee Surplus} = R_{\text{vault}} - R_{\text{cash}}
$$

This surplus represents claimable protocol revenue that flows to the futarchy governance system.

### How Fees Accumulate

**Swap Fees:**
- Swap fee is split: LP portion goes to reserves, futarchy portion goes directly to protocol
- Only the LP portion increases `cash_reserve` and `reserve`
- Futarchy fee is transferred immediately, not tracked in cash reserves

**Interest Protocol Fees:**
- When interest accrues, borrowers pay `interest + protocol_fee`
- LPs receive the full interest amount (added to virtual reserves)
- Protocol fee is tracked via the surplus mechanism

### Cash Coverage for Interest Fees

During interest accrual, the protocol handles protocol fees carefully to maintain the state transition invariant:

$$
\text{Cash Covered Fee} = \min(\text{Protocol Fee}, R_{\text{cash}})
$$

| Component | Effect | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Cash Covered Fee | Reduces $R_{\text{cash}}$ | Creates immediate surplus in vault |
| Uncovered Fee | Adds to $R_{\text{virtual}}$ | LPs get temporary claim until repayment |

The state changes ensure the invariant holds:

$$
\Delta R_{\text{virtual}} = \text{LP Share} + (\text{Protocol Fee} - \text{Cash Covered Fee})
$$
$$
\Delta R_{\text{cash}} = -\text{Cash Covered Fee}
$$
$$
\Delta R_{\text{debt}} = \text{LP Share} + \text{Protocol Fee}
$$

## Spot Price

Derived from the pool's token virtual reserves:

$$
\text{Spot Price}_{0 \rightarrow 1} = \frac{R^{\text{virtual}}_1}{R^{\text{virtual}}_0}
$$

Used for swap execution. Swaps follow the constant-product formula:

$$
\Delta y = \frac{\Delta x \cdot y}{x + \Delta x}
$$

## Double-EMA Price System

Omnipair uses a **double-EMA system** with two distinct EMAs working together for lending operations:

### 1. Symmetric EMA (Two-Way)

Standard EMA that smooths price movements in both directions:

$$
\text{EMA}_{\text{sym}}(t) = \alpha \cdot P_{\text{spot}}(t) + (1 - \alpha) \cdot \text{EMA}_{\text{sym}}(t - 1)
$$

Where $\alpha = 1 - e^{-\Delta t / \tau}$ and $\tau$ is derived from the configurable **half-life** (1 minute to 12 hours).

### 2. Directional EMA (One-Way, Asymmetric)

Designed to track price drops instantly while smoothing upward movements:

- **On price drop:** Snaps down instantly
$$
\text{EMA}_{\text{dir}} = \min(\text{EMA}_{\text{dir}}, P_{\text{spot}})
$$

- **On price rise:** Rises gradually via EMA calculation with a fixed 3-second half-life

This asymmetric behavior ensures the protocol reacts immediately to adverse price movements while preventing manipulation through temporary price spikes.

### Pessimistic Price

For all collateral factor and liquidation calculations, the protocol uses the more conservative of the two EMAs:

$$
P_{\text{pessimistic}} = \min(P_{\text{directional}}, P_{\text{symmetric}})
$$

This pessimistic price ensures:
- Rapid price drops are captured immediately via the directional EMA
- Gradual price movements use the symmetric EMA's smoothing
- Price manipulation attacks become prohibitively expensive (attacker must maintain manipulated price for ~50 slots)
